\begin{abstract}
This note documents the repository-local R230 certificate for the
nonexistence of a strongly regular graph with parameters
\(\operatorname{srg}(99,14,1,2)\).  The proof is computational: every
hypothetical graph is reduced to one of 24 rooted triangle representatives,
each representative is encoded as an exact SAT instance, and every resulting
CNF is independently checked UNSAT by DRAT proof verification.  The certificate
summary records 24 representatives, 24 UNSAT solver outputs, and 24 independent
\texttt{drat-trim} verifications.
\end{abstract}

\section{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[R230 certificate]
There is no strongly regular graph with parameters
\(\operatorname{srg}(99,14,1,2)\), assuming the correctness of the explicit
rooted reduction and the checked DRAT certificates included in this bundle.
\end{theorem}

The qualification is the standard one for a computational proof: the
mathematical reduction and the software artifacts are part of the certificate.
The goal of this bundle is to make those artifacts compact enough for
adversarial review and explicit enough for independent replay.

\section{Rooted setup}

Let \(G\) be a hypothetical \(\operatorname{srg}(99,14,1,2)\).  Root at a
vertex \(v\).  Since \(\lambda=1\), the neighborhood \(N(v)\) is a perfect
matching on 14 vertices, i.e. \(7K_2\).  The remaining 84 vertices split into
rooted far-cell fibers according to their adjacency to the matched pairs in
\(N(v)\).

The R204 reduction uses this rooted structure to replace the far-cell free-edge
surface by a finite permutation system.  In each disjoint rooted fiber block,
the common-neighbor equations force a \(4\times4\) permutation matrix.  Thus the
unknown rooted surface is represented by 105 blocks, each taking values in
\(S_4\).  The human-checkable reduction note is
\[
\texttt{reports/R204\_HUMAN\_CHECKABLE\_REDUCTION.md}.
\]
It gives the \(40=40\) covering argument that forces the permutation blocks and
records the exact symbolic equation being used.  The clean-room audit
\[
\texttt{root\_cell\_r204\_cleanroom\_symbolic\_audit.py}
\]
does not import the proof generator.  It independently rebuilds the rooted
labels and checks the symbolic reduction for every distinct-fiber far pair.  The
recorded run has \(\texttt{ok=true}\),
\(\texttt{symbolicPairEquationsChecked=3360}\), and
\(\texttt{symbolicMismatches=[]}\).  The older sampled formula audit is retained
only as a regression check.

\section{Triangle representative split}

The R220 split fixes a triangle of rooted fibers and quotients by the relevant
rooted symmetry action.  The ambient assignment space has \(24^3=13824\)
triples of \(S_4\) permutations.  The audit
\[
\texttt{root\_cell\_triangle\_orbit\_audit.py}
\]
verifies that these triples fall into exactly 24 representatives under the
\(D_8^3 \rtimes S_3\) action.  A second audit,
\[
\texttt{root\_cell\_triangle\_rep\_unit\_audit.py},
\]
checks Boolean unit propagation for the 24 fixed representatives.  It records
16 unit-dead representatives and 8 survivors:
\[
\{0,2,7,8,10,15,21,22\}.
\]

For the final certificate, all 24 representatives were nevertheless exported
and proof-checked, giving a uniform all-case certificate rather than a split
between unit and non-unit cases.

\section{Intersecting D8-coset cuts}

R229 moves the strongest compact shadow of the intersecting-fiber table into
the SAT proof surface.  For each of the 105 intersecting rooted fiber pairs,
there are six common disjoint fibers.  The exact R210 table has 35280 rows per
target.  Projecting the corresponding relative permutations to right
\(D_8\)-cosets gives 182 allowed coset rows out of \(3^6=729\), hence 547
forbidden coset patterns per intersecting pair.

The SAT encoding introduces relative-permutation literals tied back to the
original \(S_4\) block-entry variables, then introduces right-\(D_8\)-coset
literals and forbids the 547 impossible coset rows.  The audit
\[
\texttt{root\_cell\_intersecting\_coset\_sat\_audit.py}
\]
checks that this SAT-side projection matches the CP-SAT source projection and
that the relative-permutation orientation is correct over all \(24\times24\)
block pairs.  The recorded run has:
\[
\texttt{ok=true},\quad
\texttt{intersecting\_pairs=105},\quad
\texttt{allowed\_coset\_rows=182},\quad
\texttt{relative\_orientation\_failures=[]}.
\]

\section{Why the conclusion follows}

Suppose a graph \(G\) with the stated parameters exists.  Rooting at any vertex
puts \(G\) into the R204 rooted \(S_4\)-block framework.  Choosing the rooted
triangle used by R220 places the corresponding block triple into one of the 24
verified orbit representatives.  R229's extra clauses are exact consequences of
the intersecting-fiber common-neighbor table, so they do not remove any genuine
graph.  Therefore \(G\) would satisfy the CNF for one of the 24 representatives.

But every one of those 24 CNFs has an independently verified UNSAT certificate.
This contradiction proves that no such \(G\) exists, subject to the correctness
of the audited reductions and proof-checking toolchain.

\section{Limitations}

This writeup is a review note, not a polished journal proof.  It is intentionally
concrete: file names, commands, hashes, and checker outputs are part of the
argument.  A publication-grade version should archive the exact source tree,
tool builds, CNFs, DRATs, hashes, and a clean-machine independent replay.
